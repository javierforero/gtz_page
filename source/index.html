GTZ OVERVIEW

Getting to Zero is a state-wide initiative to end the HIV epidemic in Illinois by 2027. Through partnerships with community-based organizations, the Illinois department of Public Health and other non-profits throughout the state, we can see a dramatic shift to HIV in Illinois.

GOAL & VISION



We want to make sure that the HIV epidemic is no longer able to sustain itself by achieving both HIV prevention and access to care goals.

We want to see:

1. Zero new HIV infections

2. Zero people living with HIV who are not receiving treatment

Through increasing access and uptake of PrEP
(pre-exposure prophylaxis), retaining more people living with HIV in care and the continued funding of ongoing supportive services, we can get to zero.

GTZ COMMITTEES



Getting to Zero Illinois is made possible through the efforts of our five planning committees dedicated to addressing essential components of the planning process.

GTZ PLAN

The Getting to Zero Illinois plan is still in the works! We are currently in the process of evaluating community input from 9 town halls hosted in every region across the state, 5 focus groups with people most affected by HIV and over 300 online and paper surveys collected from participants interested in the Getting to Zero Illinois initiative.
